Halo campaign: at its best vs cheesing with shields at a distance

Uncharted campaign: at its best vs cheesing with regen at a distance

Dark Souls: at its best vs running past enemies

You can often see difference by looking at promos or trailers vs gameplay

Players effectively roleplay to compensate for imbalance

Sometimes these strategies require skill but work against the nature of the game, sometimes these strategies do not require engaging with many game systems (cheese)

What strategies are stimulating, challenging, fun? What experience are we aiming for?

Good design means aligning optimal strategies within the desired strategy space

Regen Health:

  • Create carrots and sticks that drive players towards action
  • Uncharted 1 had limited ammo, pushing players forward to keep stocked
  • Many Design Avenues for Halo: (some more expensive then others)

    • Combat area has shelter from wraiths / Phantoms

    • Lower Effective Ranges on Weapons

    • Higher Enemy Accuracy as Distance increases

    • Enemy Mortars that cut off in proximity

Dark Souls

  • Carrots and Sticks
  • Enemies that gatekeep environmental progression

    • Either through chokepoints

    • Or through death progression mechanism

  • Enemies that accelerate as you run
  • Long Interruptible Fog/Gate Animations (not really ideal, but cheap)
  • Running eats stamina faster -> Tired Run
  • Enemies chasing you may run into boss room (technical limitations?)
