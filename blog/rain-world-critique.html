Rain World Critique

Karma System heightens risk reward of death

  • Great concept, but also emphasizes game's design problems

Linearity Clashes with Emergent Design

  • Many regions resemble "an open world of linear paths" where progress to certain nodes is highly dependent on creature luck. (picture of nodes of ideal system++)

Camera and Pipe / Creature sensitivity responsible for most arbitrary deaths

  • Obviously frustrating, arbitrary: creates dissonance between player and creature that hampers immersion
  • Has deep design implications: disincentives open room design which worsens single path / linearity problems
  • Makes it vague to gauge if screen boundaries lead to another screen or to offworld death

Game Design Leads to Dead Ends and Traps

  • Poor Karma Gate design can lead to being trapped on the wrong end of a difficulty spike. (eg: Chimney Canopy)

    • Solutions:

    • Ensure higher difficulty side of any gate has a lower karma requirement (one that can be reached within close proximity of gate habitats)

    • Crossed Gates no longer require Karma?

    • The last Crossed Gate remains open until another gate is crossed

  • World design often leads to dead end sinks: areas that are easy to get to but incredibly difficult to get out of. (eg: Bottom of the Garbage Wastes)

    • Potential Solution: One Way tunnels: allows designers to develop escape hatches for difficult sinks without trivializing the difficulty getting there

  • Progression often demands crapshoot 'leap of faith' exploration or prior experience of the map. This clashes with the intended experience of emergent survival
  • Prey respawn seems poorly balanced with Karma / Death. Low Karma Slugcats can have a very difficult time finding food in a long term habitat.

Criticism of Matthewmatosis appeal to 'nature is cruel'

  • Cruel deaths that are not a product of the emergent ecosystem but are a product of arbitrary design decisions or stifling linearity do not develop the experience of an unforgiving hostile ecosystem. They instead develop a greater awareness of design shortcomings. They detract from the player's sense of place and immersion rather than build it.
