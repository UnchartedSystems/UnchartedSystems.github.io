Souls Critique

  • Game developed around its own conventions rather than progressing and changing conventions in pursuit of the initial intended experience
  • As a result, fights tended towards more and more perfection, more bonfires as compensation, particularily bonfires in front of bosses
  • This is in contrast to the original vision set out by Demon Souls and to a lesser extent Dark Souls, where bossfights concluded the end of the level. Traversing the level itself often took up the brunt of playtime and difficulty.

    • Bosses compounded the need for mastery over the exploration of the level, as improvements over the exploration lead to a better position at the start of the boss, the boss was an extension of performance over the level.

  • Over time, as bonfires have been more prevalent and in front of bosses, the mechanical execution required for the boss itself has shot up to an absurd degree.
  • Meanwhile, exploration requires design work to prevent rampant cheese of running? sidenote?
  • Time spent learning and playing the boss has risen dramatically relative to time spent exploring and learning the level as a whole
  • Meta progression has constantly demanded more mechanical precision, we have gone from a set of mechanics designed to emulate a certain action adventure experience, to an experience that has become too self referential, and is based on abusing i-frame windows in rolls to exploit precise attack patterns

  • Parallel to Super Meat Boy

  • Sekiro is slightly refreshing, same rote memorization and mismatch between the emphasis on bosses vs exploration, but on other hand, combat feels more aggressive, not sure about this?
